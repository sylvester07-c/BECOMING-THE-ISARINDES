Ìtàn Wa · Our Story

Ẹni tó rí iyàwó rí ohun rere

"Not a girlfriend that needed to become a wife, a wife, waiting to be found."

Okay so let me tell you how this whole thing started.

It was August 2021. I had gone to OAU Ile-Ife to teach at a Bible seminar, just doing my thing, you know how it is. And somewhere in that gathering was this young woman, Goodnews Aderinola. Ajoke. Omo Afe. She was there, sitting, paying attention. Nothing dramatic happened that day o. We just ended up in the same reading club after, going through one of those Puritan books, the kind that makes you feel like you need to sit down and rethink your life. And I noticed her. Quietly. I'm not saying anything more than that.

Fast forward a little, we had our first video call on the 29th of August. Her birthday, by the way. She was in Akure, I was in Lagos doing NYSC, and we were just... talking. Nothing serious. Just two people who knew each other small. We'd check in once in a while, nothing that would make anybody start printing aso-ebi.

Then November 2022 happened and e don do.

Lagos Bible Conference. After dinner, a bunch of us were just sitting around gisting, and somehow the conversation moved to marriage, God's design, what a wife's role looks like, what a husband should be. And this woman opened her mouth and spoke with a kind of conviction that made me go quiet. Not the kind of thing you say to impress somebody. Not the kind of thing you rehearse. She had clearly sat with God on these things and settled them long before I showed up. There was no performance. No packaging. Just her.

I want to be honest with you, in that moment, two things landed in my spirit and they have not moved since. One, her faith is the real thing. Two, this is not a girl I need to work on or grow into a wife. This is a wife. Already. Just waiting to be found.

Now, how exactly I went from that dinner conversation to where we are today, that story is a little longer and I will not be sharing all of it here. You'll need to subscribe to my newsletter for that one. What I will say is this, God has been very deliberate about this, and on the 12th of September 2026, we want you there with us as we step into everything He has written for us.

Ìrìn-àjò · Getting Here

Your Journey to Akure

Whether you are coming from Lagos, Ekiti, or Abuja — we have mapped out the easiest route for you. Once in Akure, head towards Sijuade, off Ala Close for both the event venues and the recommended hotel.

Lagos Akure (~300 km)
By Road (4–5 hours): Lagos → Sagamu Interchange → Ore → Akure via the Benin–Ore–Akure Expressway.

Bus Companies: GUO Transport, ABC Transport, GIGM (God is Good Motors), Peace Mass Transit. Depart from Ojota, Jibowu, or Mile 2 motor parks in Lagos.

Book online: gigm.com · intercity.ng · PalmPay or OPay apps.

Private Car: Most comfortable. Budget ₦20,000–₦35,000 for fuel depending on vehicle.
We strongly recommend travelling to Akure on Friday, September 11 — the morning of the 12th can be busy on this route, and the traditional ceremony starts at 7:00 AM.
Ado-Ekiti Akure (~80 km)
By Road (~1.5–2 hours): Ado-Ekiti → Aramoko-Ekiti → Akure Road → Akure.

Options: Commercial vehicles at the Ado-Ekiti central park. This is the shortest journey of all three cities.

Cost: Approximately ₦2,000–₦4,000 by commercial bus.
Ekiti guests, you are the closest! You still have the option of coming the night before to enjoy the full day without rushing — especially as the traditional ceremony begins at 7:00 AM.
Abuja Akure (~620 km)
By Air (Recommended): Fly Abuja (ABV) → Lagos (LOS), then connect or drive to Akure. Alternatively, direct flights to Akure Airport (AKR) via Overland Airways or Air Peace.

By Road (7–9 hours): Abuja → Lokoja → Okene → Akure. GUO Transport and Peace Mass Transit operate from Utako/Nyanya parks in Abuja.

Tip: Book flights early — Akure Airport is small and seats fill up quickly for wedding weekends.
Abuja guests: We strongly recommend arriving Thursday or Friday. With a 7:00 AM traditional ceremony, a same-day journey from Abuja is not advisable.
Any City Akure Airport (AKR)
Akure Airport (AKR) — Oba-Ile Road, Akure, Ondo State.

Airlines: Overland Airways (from Lagos) · Air Peace (from Lagos) · Green Africa Airways.

Flight time from Lagos: Approximately 45–60 minutes.

From the airport to venues: Taxis cost approximately ₦3,000–₦6,000. Download Bolt before arriving — it works well in Akure.
Download the Bolt app before you land. It is cheaper and more reliable than negotiating with taxis at the airport.
OAU Students Akure
For OAU Students:

Please contact Sekinah Akinniyi, PG Architecture, or The Food Architect (TFA) for all information about transport, student's aso-ebi, and accommodation purposes.
Reach out to them early to secure your spot for group transport and get your aso-ebi sorted!
From Airport / Garage Event Venues
General Direction: Once you arrive in Akure — whether by road or air — ask your driver or Bolt to head towards Sijuade, off Ala Close, Akure. Both St. Luke's Anglican Church and RCCG Higher Ground Cathedral are in this area.

From Akure Main Garage (Old Garage / Fanibi): Take a cab heading toward Sijuade Estate. Journey is approximately 10–15 minutes.

From Akure Airport: Take Bolt or airport taxi. Tell the driver: "Sijuade, off Ala Close" — approximately 15–20 minutes.

Use Google Maps: Links for both venues are in the Events section above. Save them offline before you travel.
